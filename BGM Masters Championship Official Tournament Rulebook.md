## **SECTION 1 \- EVENT OVERVIEW & EVENT INFORMATION**

### **Event Overview**

The BC Game Masters Championship is a standalone Counter-Strike 2 LAN tournament organized by VANTA.GL (FZ-C.L.L VANTAGL, License No: 2643721.01, Address: Meydan Grandstand, 6th Floor, Meydan Road, Nad Al Sheba, Dubai, U.A.E.), operating as an independent competitive esports event.

BC Game Masters Championship is a Valve Regional Standings (VRS)-eligible Tier 2 tournament, structured to comply with Valve’s tournament operation guidelines for ranked events. Match results from the event contribute toward Valve’s official VRS rankings, which are used to evaluate team performance and eligibility for future CS2 competitions.

The event is designed as an open-registration LAN tournament, welcoming professional and semi-professional teams from the Global region. While registration is open to all eligible teams, competitive seeding is determined using Valve’s official VRS rankings to ensure a balanced and fair tournament structure.

This rulebook defines all rules, formats, administrative procedures, and conduct requirements applicable to teams participating in the BC Game Masters Championship. All teams, players, and staff are expected to read and comply fully with the contents of this document.

## **SECTION 4 \- TOURNAMENT FORMAT**

### **Overview of Tournament Stages**

The BC Game Masters Championship is played over multiple days and consists of two primary competitive phases:

1. Group Stage

2. Playoffs (Main Event)

With a maximum of 24 registered teams, the tournament format is structured to combine open competition with VRS-based seeding while maintaining a clear and efficient progression path.

### **Stage 1 \- Group Stage**

#### Participants

* 16 teams participate in the Group Stage.

* These are all registered teams excluding the top eight (8) teams by Valve Regional Standings (VRS), which are seeded directly into the Playoffs.

#### Group Formation

* Teams are divided into four (4) GSL-style groups, each consisting of four (4) teams.

* Groups are formed using a seeded draw system, based on competitive strength.

Seeding Pots

Teams are allocated into four pots by the organizer, using the following criteria:

* Valve VRS ranking

* HLTV ranking (where applicable)

* Recent competitive results and performance

Each group contains exactly one team from each pot.

#### Group Format (GSL)

Each group follows a GSL (double-elimination within group) format:

* Opening Matches: Best-of-1

* Winners’ Match: Best-of-1

* Elimination Match: Best-of-3

* Decider Match: Best-of-3

All matches use overtime to ensure a decisive result. Draws are not permitted.

#### Advancement from Groups

* The Group Winner and Group Runner-up advance to the Playoffs.

* A total of eight (8) teams qualify from the Group Stage.

Teams finishing third or fourth in their groups are eliminated from the tournament.

#### Group Stage Scheduling & Readiness

* Group Stage matches are played across available LAN setups in scheduled waves.

* Teams must be ready on the server at least 15 minutes before their scheduled match time.

* Short breaks (approximately 5–10 minutes) may be provided between consecutive matches.

* Teams playing Decider Matches must be prepared for limited downtime between matches.

### **Stage 2 \- Playoffs (Main Event)**

#### Participants

The Playoffs consist of sixteen (16) teams:

* 8 teams qualified from the Group Stage

* 8 teams seeded directly based on Valve VRS rankings (March snapshot)

#### Seeding into Playoffs

* The top eight (8) registered teams according to the March Valve VRS rankings are directly seeded into the Playoffs.

* The 8 VRS-seeded teams and the 8 Group Stage qualifier teams are paired in the Opening Playoff Round (BO1) as follows:

| Match \# | VRS-Seeded Team | Group Stage Position |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Match 1 | VRS \#1 | 4th Ranked Group Stage Runner-up |
| Match 2 | VRS \#8 | 1st Ranked Group Stage Winner |
| Match 3 | VRS \#4 | 1st Ranked Group Stage Runner-up |
| Match 4 | VRS \#5 | 4th Ranked Group Stage Winner |
| Match 5 | VRS \#6 | 3rd Ranked Group Stage Winner |
| Match 6 | VRS \#3 | 2nd Ranked Group Stage Runner-up |
| Match 7 | VRS \#7 | 2nd Ranked Group Stage Winner |
| Match 8 | VRS \#2 | 3rd Ranked Group Stage Runner-up  |

**Playoff Seeding Clarification**

The top eight (8) teams according to the official Valve Regional Standings (VRS) February ranking will be seeded directly into the Playoffs as Seeds 1–8.

Seeds 9–12 will be the teams that finish first in their respective Group Stage groups. Seeds 13–16 will be the teams that finish second in their groups.

The internal order of Seeds 9–16 will be determined using the same criteria applied during the initial tournament seeding:

1. Valve Regional Standings (VRS) ranking

2. HLTV ranking

3. Average FACEIT ELO (if no VRS ranking is available)

This seeding order will determine playoff matchups and veto priority.

#### Playoff Bracket Format

* Single-Elimination Bracket

* Teams are eliminated after one match loss.

* There is no lower bracket.

#### Match Format

* All Playoff matches are played as Best-of-3 (Bo3) series.

* This includes:

  * Round of 16

  * Quarterfinals

  * Semifinals

  * Grand Final

### **Grand Final**

* The Grand Final is played as a Best-of-3 (Bo3) series.

* There is:

  * No bracket reset

  * No map advantage

* The higher-seeded team is determined by tournament seeding but does not receive competitive advantages beyond standard veto priority.

* The winner of the Grand Final is crowned BC Game Masters Championship Champion.

### **General Match Rules**

* Overtime is enabled for all matches.

* Best-of-1 and Best-of-3 matches follow the same overtime rules as defined in the Match Settings section.

* Half-time breaks are three (3) minutes.

* Forfeits and no-shows are handled under the Conduct & Administration sections of this rulebook.

## **SECTION 5 \- MATCH SCHEDULING & PUNCTUALITY**

### **Match Schedule**

A detailed match schedule, including approximate start times for each round, will be provided to all teams prior to the event and made available through official tournament communication channels.

All teams are expected to follow the published schedule closely. Matches are intended to begin at their scheduled start times. Any deviation from the schedule must be approved by tournament administrators.

### **Pre-Match Readiness**

Teams must be fully ready at least fifteen (15) minutes before their scheduled match start time.

Being “ready” means:

* All five (5) players are present at the venue

* All players are logged into the match server or designated staging area

* Equipment is set up and functional

* Players are prepared to begin the match immediately upon admin instruction

Failure to meet readiness requirements may result in penalties.

### **Late Arrival & Grace Period**

If a team is not fully ready at the scheduled match start time:

* A grace period of up to ten (10) minutes may be granted at the discretion of tournament administrators.

* After the grace period expires, the administrators may:

  * Award a map forfeit, or

  * Declare a match forfeit, depending on the situation and tournament phase.

A complete no-show (failure to appear) will result in an automatic match loss by forfeit.

### **Breaks & Downtime**

To maintain tournament flow and scheduling integrity:

* Teams are allowed up to ten (10) minutes between maps in a Best-of-3 series.

* Teams are allowed up to thirty (30) minutes between consecutive matches.

* If a preceding match runs longer than expected, subsequent breaks may be shortened at admin discretion.

Teams with back-to-back matches must remain available in the tournament area unless explicitly granted permission by an admin to leave.

### **Schedule Adjustments**

Tournament administrators reserve the right to adjust match start times, reorder matches, and shorten or extend breaks.

These adjustments may be made to accommodate delays, technical issues, broadcast requirements, or venue constraints. Teams are expected to remain flexible and cooperative to ensure the smooth operation of the event.

Failure to comply with scheduling instructions may result in penalties.

## **SECTION 6 \- GAME RULES & MATCH SETTINGS**

### **General Game Settings**

All matches in the BC Game Masters Championship are played using Counter-Strike 2 on the latest available version on Steam at the time of the event.

* Game Title: Counter-Strike 2

* Platform: LAN servers provided by the tournament organizer

* Server Type: Offline LAN environment

* Anti-Cheat:

  * No third-party anti-cheat client is required

  * Tournament administrators will actively monitor all matches for suspicious behavior

All matches will be played using the official CS2 competitive configuration. Server settings will remain unchanged for the duration of the tournament, regardless of any updates released during the event.

### **Player Accounts**

* Players must compete using their own personal Steam / CS2 accounts.

* Account sharing is strictly prohibited.

* All accounts must:

  * Own Counter-Strike 2

  * Be in good standing

  * Have no VAC bans or active competitive bans

Violation of account rules may result in immediate disqualification.

### **Match Parameters**

* Match Format: MR12

* Winning Condition: First team to reach 13 rounds wins the map

* Maximum Regulation Rounds: 24 (12 per half)

**Standard Match Settings**

* Round Time: 1 minute 55 seconds

* Bomb Timer: 40 seconds

* Freeze Time: 20 seconds

* Buy Time: 20 seconds

* Starting Money (Pistol Round): $800

### **Overtime Rules**

If a map ends 12–12 after regulation:

* Overtime will be played to determine a winner

* Overtime Format: MR3

* Start Money: $10,000

* Teams play:

  * 3 rounds per side

  * Swap sides after 3 rounds

If the score remains tied after one overtime, additional overtime periods will be played under the same rules until a winner is determined.

### **Side & Map Selection**

#### First Map of a Match

The higher-seeded team may choose one of the following:

* a) Map veto order

* b) Starting side

#### Subsequent Maps in a Best-of-3

* When a team picks a map, the opposing team selects the starting side.

* Side selection follows the veto process.

#### **Overtime Side Rules**

* Teams begin overtime on the side they finished regulation.

* Sides switch after three overtime rounds.

* Overtime repeats until a winner is decided.

### **Map Pool**

The tournament uses the official Active Duty map pool at the time of the event.

Current map pool consists of the following seven (7) maps:

* Inferno

* Mirage

* Nuke

* Ancient

* Overpass

* Dust2

* Anubis

If Valve updates the Active Duty pool prior to the event, the updated pool will be used.

### **Seeding Determination**

All participating teams are seeded prior to the tournament by the organizer.

Seeding is determined using:

* Valve Regional Standings (VRS)

* HLTV rankings (where applicable)

* Recent competitive performance and results

Teams are allocated into seeding pots, with higher-ranked teams receiving higher priority.

In all matches, the higher-seeded team receives veto priority and side selection rights as defined in this rulebook.

### **Map Veto Process**

The veto process is supervised by tournament administrators and may be conducted:

* On-stage

* Through the game server

* Via an approved veto tool

Teams must make their veto decisions promptly. A time limit of approximately 1–2 minutes per decision may be enforced.

#### **Best-of-1 (Bo1) Veto Process**

1. Higher-seeded team chooses to be Team A or Team B

2. Team A removes 2 maps

3. Team B removes 3 maps

4. Team A removes 1 map

5. Remaining map is played

6. Team B chooses starting side

#### **Best-of-3 (Bo3) Veto Process**

1. Higher-seeded team chooses to be Team A or Team B

2. Team A removes 1 map

3. Team B removes 1 map

4. Team A picks Map 1

5. Team B chooses starting side on Map 1

6. Team B picks Map 2

7. Team A chooses starting side on Map 2

8. Team B removes 1 map

9. Team A removes 1 map

10. Remaining map becomes the decider (Map 3\)

11. Team B chooses starting side on Map 3

### **Match Server & Configuration**

* Matches are hosted on tournament-provided LAN servers.

* Teams are given a short setup window before matches to:

  * Execute configs

  * Adjust settings

  * Test peripherals

Once both teams signal readiness, the match goes live. Any configuration issues not raised before the first round begins are considered accepted.

## **SECTION 7 \- PAUSES, INTERRUPTIONS & MATCH RECOVERY**

### **Tactical Pauses (Timeouts)**

* Each team is allowed three (3) tactical timeouts per map.

* An additional one (1) tactical timeout is granted per team in overtime.

* Each tactical timeout lasts 30 seconds.

* Tactical timeouts may be used consecutively if desired.

Tactical timeouts are intended solely for team discussion and strategic adjustments. Teams must use the in-game timeout functionality to initiate them.

Once a team has exhausted its allotted tactical timeouts for a map, no further tactical pauses are permitted for that map.

### **Technical Pauses**

If a player encounters a legitimate technical issue (hardware malfunction, disconnect, severe lag):

* The affected player must immediately notify the server by typing “\!tech” in chat.

* The game should be paused as soon as possible.

**During a technical pause:**

* Players, coaches, and team staff must remain at their stations.

* No game-related communication is permitted.

* Coaches are not allowed to speak.

Abuse of technical pauses may result in penalties.

### **Admin Pauses**

Tournament administrators may pause a match at any time to:

* Resolve technical issues

* Address disputes

* Handle server or venue-related problems

* Prevent competitive integrity issues

**During an admin pause:**

* All participants must follow admin instructions

* No game-related communication is allowed

* Coaches must remain silent

### **Duration of Pauses**

* Tactical timeouts are fixed at 30 seconds unless chained.

* Technical pauses last only as long as required to resolve the issue.

* If a technical issue cannot be resolved within a reasonable time frame (generally up to 10 minutes), administrators may:

  * Force the match to continue, or

  * Declare a forfeit if a team is unable to continue playing

Teams are expected to have backup equipment available to minimize delays.

### **Match Interruptions & Recovery**

The BC Game Masters Championship aims to ensure match integrity in the event of interruptions.

#### **Server Crash or Full Disconnect**

If the game server crashes or all players disconnect simultaneously:

* The round in progress is voided and replayed

* The match will be restored to the last fully completed round

* CS2 match recovery tools (“match medic”) will be used where available.

External communication, including phone usage or stream viewing, is strictly prohibited during recovery.

**Player Disconnects**

* If a player disconnects early in a round before any significant action, admins may order a round restart.

* Significant action includes:

  * Damage dealt

  * Kills

  * Utility usage

  * Revealing strategic information

If significant action has occurred, the round continues with remaining players.

Admins reserve the right to replay a round if multiple disconnects or severe issues materially impact the outcome.

#### **No Replay for Team Errors**

Rounds will not be replayed for:

* Misbuys

* Configuration mistakes

* Accidental disconnects caused by player error

Such situations are considered part of normal competitive risk.

### **Match Medic Usage**

Tournament administrators may use CS2’s match restoration tools to:

* Restore scorelines

* Restore economy states where possible

If full restoration is not feasible, admins may restore the score only and resume play under standard conditions.

A full map restart will only occur in extreme cases where competitive integrity cannot otherwise be preserved.

### **Map Bugs & Exploits**

The intentional use of bugs, glitches, or exploits is strictly prohibited, including but not limited to:

* Clipping through map geometry

* Pixel walking on unintended surfaces

* Abusive boosts that allow unintended sightlines

* Scripted movement exploits

When in doubt, teams must ask administrators for clarification. Exploits that clearly violate fair play principles will be penalized even if not explicitly listed.

## **SECTION 9 \- CONDUCT, FAIR PLAY & COMPETITION INTEGRITY**

### **Fair Play & Sportsmanship**

All participants in the BC Game Masters Championship are expected to compete with integrity, professionalism, and respect toward opponents, officials, staff, and spectators.

Unsportsmanlike behavior, abuse, or actions that undermine the competitive environment will not be tolerated.

### **Cheating**

Any form of cheating is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate disqualification.

**Cheating includes, but is not limited to:**

* Use of external software or hacks (aimbots, wallhacks, radar hacks, trigger bots, no-flash, etc.)

* Unauthorized game modifications

* Hardware-based cheating or macros

* Exploiting software vulnerabilities for unfair advantage

Tournament administrators will actively monitor matches. VAC-enabled servers and admin observation are used to detect violations.

**Players found cheating may be:**

* Disqualified from the event

* Banned from future events organized by the organizer

* Reported to Valve, which may result in further competitive sanctions

Entire teams may be disqualified depending on the severity of the violation.

### **Ghosting & Outside Assistance**

Because the tournament is played on LAN, ghosting is strictly forbidden.

* Players may not receive any outside information during a live match.

* Spectators, eliminated players, or staff may not communicate match information to active players.

* Coaches may only communicate within the limits defined by Valve rules.

Any attempt to relay live match information will result in penalties for both the individual and the team involved.

### **Exploits & Bug Abuse**

Deliberate abuse of game bugs, glitches, or unintended mechanics is prohibited.

**This includes:**

* Clipping through map geometry

* Pixel walking or standing on unintended surfaces

* Boosts that allow unintended sightlines

* Exploiting newly discovered bugs for advantage

If a bug’s legality is unclear, teams must ask administrators before using it. Using an exploit deemed illegal may result in:

* Round forfeits

* Map forfeits

* Match forfeits

* Disqualification, depending on severity

### **Forbidden Scripts & Configurations**

Players may only use configurations and scripts allowed in standard competitive CS2 play.

**Allowed:**

* Jump-throw binds

* Buy binds

* Standard aliases

**Forbidden:**

* Recoil scripts

* Rapid-fire macros

* Movement automation

* Any script or macro providing mechanical advantage

Forbidden scripts are illegal even if not actively used. Software-based macros via peripheral drivers are also prohibited.

### **In-Game Communication & Behavior**

* All-chat should be used sparingly and respectfully.

* Harassment, excessive profanity, hate speech, or discriminatory language is prohibited.

* Spamming, taunting, or disruptive behavior may result in penalties.

Players are expected to behave professionally at all times, including during wins and losses.

### **Player Conduct**

Unsportsmanlike conduct includes, but is not limited to:

* Harassment or bullying

* Verbal or physical aggression

* Throwing equipment

* Disrespecting staff or officials

Severe incidents may result in immediate disqualification and removal from the venue.

### **Compliance with Officials**

Tournament administrators and referees have full authority during the event.

* Teams must follow all admin instructions promptly.

* Excessive arguing, refusal to comply, or attempts to mislead officials are considered misconduct.

If a team wishes to dispute a ruling, the team captain must raise the issue calmly with an administrator at an appropriate time.

### **Rule Enforcement & Penalties**

Administrators have final authority over:

* Rule interpretation

* Match results

* Disputes and penalties

**Possible penalties include:**

* Warnings

* Round loss

* Map loss

* Match forfeiture

* Disqualification

* Event bans

Penalties will be proportional to the offense.

### **Protests**

* Protests must be raised immediately when an issue occurs.

* Issues should not be raised after a match if they were evident during play.

* Admin decisions after review are final.

Judgment calls are not grounds for prolonged dispute.

### **Match Integrity & Match-Fixing**

Any attempt to:

* Fix matches

* Collude with other teams

* Intentionally lose

* Manipulate results

is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate disqualification and potential reporting to Valve.

### **Definition of Collusion**

Collusion refers to any agreement or coordinated conduct between two or more teams, players, or associated parties intended to manipulate match results, influence tournament standings, gain competitive advantage, or alter prize distribution.

Collusion may be established through direct or indirect evidence and does not require that the intended outcome was successfully achieved.

### **Betting**

Players, coaches, and team staff are not allowed to bet on any match in the tournament.

Any involvement in betting-related misconduct will result in removal from the event.
